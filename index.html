↗ Session Script
15 Minutes Before Session
  • Play Yoga Padhi music
  • Set up the Unfollow Wall with sticky notes and markers
  • Keep QR codes and standees ready and visible
  • Volunteers welcome participants and engage in light, natural interaction
Unfollow Wall

As participants walk in, volunteers invite them:

"Hello. You are welcome to take a moment to write one thing you would like to unfollow today. It could be something that feels unnecessary, distracting, or something you are simply ready to let go of."

Optional prompts if needed: Overthinking · Constant distraction · Comparison · Academic or work stress

Allow participants to write without rush. No announcement yet.

Waiting Announcement
"Good Morning / Good Evening everyone. Welcome to today's session. We will wait for a couple of minutes for everyone to join. In the meantime, those who didn't get a chance to post in the unfollow wall can do so now. Also, you can keep your phone on silent mode. We will begin shortly."
Welcome & Introduction
"Good Morning / Good Evening everyone. My name is ______. I am a student / working professional. I'm also a volunteer with Isha Foundation founded by Sadhguru. Sadhguru has been offering tools for wellbeing and self-transformation rooted in the science of yoga that have touched the lives of millions of people worldwide over the past four decades.

Today, we'll be learning a simple yet powerful meditation designed by Sadhguru — that can help you to experience the Miracle of the Mind, to take charge of your mental wellbeing and explore your peak potential."
Interaction
  • "Do you feel your thoughts are noisy sometimes?"
  • "Have you wished you could just mute them for a while so you could focus on something?"
  • "How many times have we tried to control our thoughts, and it didn't work?"

Pause — allow them to respond

Introduce Miracle of Mind

Move hand in an unsteady manner

"There is a whole science and technology with which you can take charge of your mind and explore its infinite potential. It is both possible and simple; it just needs a little attention and care. Shall we explore this meditation called Miracle of Mind, which can assist you in this direction?

Sadhguru will first explain the science behind the Miracle of Mind meditation, and then guide us through it."
Introduction to Practice
"Miracle of Mind is a free, 7-minute guided meditation by Sadhguru — a yogi, author, and humanitarian who brings tools from ancient yogic science into a practical, modern-day context. It's simple, requires no prior experience, and can be practiced anytime, anywhere.

Many people skip meditation because they think it only works after years of daily practice. It's not true! A new study tracked the exact timing of brainwave changes during meditation. Significant neurophysiological effects begin within 2–3 minutes and peak at 7 minutes, even for beginners. Just like exercise supports the body, this meditation helps bring clarity and stability to the mind."
Guided Meditation
"Now, are we ready to give it a try? Sadhguru will explain the science behind this meditation and guide you through the steps."

How can I encourage my colleagues and staff to get into meditation?
Share your own experience of practicing it, refer them to the published research studies, and encourage them to download the app and try it on their own.
Is the app completely free?
Yes. This meditation is available on the Miracle of Mind app and it's free for life.
How should one sit when doing this meditation?
You can sit in a comfortable cross-legged posture or even on a chair.
Can I do this while I'm traveling?
Yes, you can practice this meditation anytime, anywhere. Just ensure there are no interruptions for those 7 minutes.
Can this be practiced at any time of the day?
Yes, it can be practiced any time of the day.
Is there a limit to the number of times I can do this meditation?
You can do this as many times as you wish. If you have more time, you can also meditate for an extended period using the app: 12, 15, 18, or 21 minutes.
Is this safe for anyone to practice?
Yes, it's safe for anyone. Anyone over 6 years of age can do this meditation and reap its benefits.
Can pregnant women do this meditation?
Yes, pregnant women can do this practice — it can be a wonderful support during this phase.
Can this be practiced by patients with mental health conditions?
Yes, as long as they are able to follow the instructions and comfortably sit through the full 7-minute duration.
Is there a way to get in touch with your organization if I have further questions?
You can reach out through our support portal: support.ishafoundation.org

Purpose

The Unfollow Wall is a simple, interactive experience that invites people to pause and notice what they may be carrying mentally — a thought, worry, or pattern they don't want to keep engaging with. It offers an easy, relatable entry point into the idea of creating a little space from the mind.

How to Introduce It

Keep it light, simple, and conversational:

"This is our Unfollow Wall — kind of like social media, but for your mind."
"Is there anything you've been thinking about or dealing with that you'd like to unfollow, even just for today?"
"It could be stress, overthinking, pressure… anything that's been on repeat."

No need to go deep unless they want to.

Participant Experience
  • Write one thing they want to "unfollow" on a sticky note
  • Place it on the wall

Quick, personal, and engaging — that's it.

Bridging to Meditation

After they participate, gently connect the experience:

"Sometimes it's hard to just stop thinking about something, right?"
"Meditation can help create a little space between you and your thoughts."
"So instead of being pulled by everything happening in your mind, you can just see it more clearly."

Optional: "We have a 7-minute guided meditation here if you'd like to try it."

Volunteer Guidance
  • Keep the tone casual and welcoming
  • This is not about fixing anything, just noticing and stepping back
  • Let participants share as much or as little as they want
  • Avoid over-explaining. The experience should feel natural
  • Use this as a soft entry point into the meditation experience
Outcome
  • Creates immediate engagement
  • Helps people feel they are not alone in their experience
  • Opens the door to a deeper, experiential understanding through meditation
