Unfollow Wall

As participants walk in, volunteers invite them:

"Hello. You are welcome to take a moment to write one thing you would like to unfollow today. It could be something that feels unnecessary, distracting, or something you are simply ready to let go of."

Optional prompts if needed: Overthinking · Constant distraction · Comparison · Academic or work stress

Allow participants to write without rush. No announcement yet.

Waiting Announcement
"Good Morning / Good Evening everyone. Welcome to today's session. We will wait for a couple of minutes for everyone to join. In the meantime, those who didn't get a chance to post in the unfollow wall can do so now. Also, you can keep your phone on silent mode. We will begin shortly."

"Good Morning / Good Evening everyone. My name is ______. I am a student / working professional. I'm also a volunteer with Isha Foundation founded by Sadhguru. Sadhguru has been offering tools for wellbeing and self-transformation rooted in the science of yoga that have touched the lives of millions of people worldwide over the past four decades.

Today, we'll be learning a simple yet powerful meditation designed by Sadhguru — that can help you to experience the Miracle of the Mind, to take charge of your mental wellbeing and explore your peak potential."

Guidelines
  • To be conducted by a trained Ishanga (FO / Satsang / IE)
  • Can be offered in any setting where there is a gathering of potential participants (non-meditators)
  • May be conducted spontaneously, as long as the group is willing and a conducive ambience can be created
  • Space should be calm and minimally distracting, with limited movement of people
  • Seating flexible — chairs or floor, whatever is available
  • Essential: portable speaker for clear audio
  • Preferred: TV screen or projector
  • Participants are requested to stay for the full duration (~15 minutes)
Suitable Events
  • Punya Poojas, private family/community gatherings
  • Any public event where people can commit 15 minutes

How can I encourage my colleagues and staff to get into meditation?
Share your own experience of practicing it, refer them to the published research studies, and encourage them to download the app and try it on their own.
Is the app completely free?
Yes. This meditation is available on the Miracle of Mind app and it's free for life.
How should one sit when doing this meditation?
You can sit in a comfortable cross-legged posture or even on a chair.
Can I do this while I'm traveling?
Yes, you can practice this meditation anytime, anywhere. Just ensure there are no interruptions for those 7 minutes.
Can this be practiced at any time of the day?
Yes, it can be practiced any time of the day.
Is there a limit to the number of times I can do this meditation?
You can do this as many times as you wish. If you have more time, you can also meditate for an extended period using the app: 12, 15, 18, or 21 minutes.
Is this safe for anyone to practice?
Yes, it's safe for anyone. Anyone over 6 years of age can do this meditation and reap its benefits.
Can pregnant women do this meditation?
Yes, pregnant women can do this practice — it can be a wonderful support during this phase.
Can this be practiced by patients with mental health conditions?
Yes, as long as they are able to follow the instructions and comfortably sit through the full 7-minute duration.
Is there a way to get in touch with your organization if I have further questions?
You can reach out through our support portal: support.ishafoundation.org

1 · Pre-Session Setup

Create a conducive atmosphere that helps participants become receptive.

  • Play Yoga Padhi music approximately 15 minutes before the session start time
  • Set up the Unfollow Wall with sticky notes and markers
  • Keep QR codes and standees ready and visible
  • Volunteers welcome participants and engage in a natural interaction
2 · Unfollow Wall

As participants walk in, volunteers invite them:

  • You are welcome to take a moment to write one thing you would like to unfollow today
  • It could be something that feels unnecessary, distracting, or something you are simply ready to let go of
  • Optional prompts: Overthinking · Constant distraction · Comparison · Academic or work stress
  • Allow participants to write without rush — no announcement yet
3 · Waiting & Opening
  • Welcome participants at the announced start time
  • As a courtesy, ask if it's okay to wait briefly for late comers — no more than 5 minutes
  • Invite those who have not used the Unfollow Wall to do so
  • Guide participants to keep phones on Silent / Do Not Disturb and avoid getting up during meditation
4 · Introduction

Introduce yourself as a volunteer with Isha Foundation.

  • Keep the introduction natural and appropriate to the audience
  • Share your name, profession, and brief personal details that help you connect and be relatable
  • Introduce Isha Foundation and Sadhguru
  • Briefly mention the offering of tools for wellbeing and self-transformation rooted in the science of yoga
5 · Engaging Participants

Create curiosity, awareness, and interest in mental well-being.

  • Ask simple, relatable questions about noisy thoughts, lack of control, and the desire to do something about it
  • Demonstrate an uncontrolled hand movement to illustrate how the mind can act without our control
  • Help participants relate by comparing uncontrolled thoughts to hands moving on their own
  • Emphasize that there is a science and technology through which one can take charge of the mind and explore its potential
6 · Introduce Miracle of Mind App

A simple yet powerful tool for mental well-being.

  • Just 7 minutes a day can improve mental health, like how 10,000 steps benefit the body
  • Combines scientific backing and ancient wisdom — easily accessible anywhere
  • Empowers participants to take charge of their own mind
  • Address the perception that meditation requires a lot of time
  • Studies indicate neurophysiological effects begin within 2–3 minutes and peak at around 7 minutes
7 · Play Sadhguru Video & Guided Meditation
  • Play the main Sadhguru video that explains the science behind meditation
  • Continue with the full guided meditation — no interruptions
▶ Duration: 7 minutes for meditation
8 · Participant Sharing
  • Invite participants to share their experience (e.g., peaceful, calm, quiet)
  • Acknowledge that some may feel their mind was still restless — normalize all experiences
  • Take a few sharings and allow participants to reorient themselves to the screen
9 · Sadhguru Video on Thoughts During Meditation
  • Introduce the topic of handling thoughts during meditation
▶ Play Sadhguru video — how to manage thoughts during meditation
10 · Participant Experience Video
▶ Play short video of participant experiences to reinforce relatability
11 · Sadhguru & Isha Foundation Intro  ·  Optional
  • Briefly introduce Sadhguru as a yogi and New York Times bestselling author focused on human wellbeing
  • Share about his work in offering tools for transformation and involvement in various initiatives
  • Position Miracle of Mind as a simple and accessible offering for everyday use
▶ Play Sadhguru introduction video (optional, based on audience context)
12 · App Download & Spread

Empower participants to help others benefit and spread mental well-being.

  • Display the QR code for the Miracle of Mind app
  • Encourage participants to download and continue the practice
  • Highlight key features: AI Q&A support · Adjustable pace · Hopscotch · Bite-sized wisdom
  • Encourage participants to share it with friends, family, and colleagues
  • Volunteers support participants in downloading the app
13 · Customizing for Audience

Make the session relatable and conversational to increase engagement.

  • Friends & Family: No need for a formal introduction — connect to your personal journey
  • Colleagues: Relate to workplace challenges, stress management, and productivity
  • General tip: Keep the tone conversational, relatable, and natural to maintain engagement

Purpose

The Unfollow Wall is a simple, interactive experience that invites people to pause and notice what they may be carrying mentally — a thought, worry, or pattern they don't want to keep engaging with. It offers an easy, relatable entry point into the idea of creating a little space from the mind.

How to Introduce It

Keep it light, simple, and conversational:

"This is our Unfollow Wall — kind of like social media, but for your mind."
"Is there anything you've been thinking about or dealing with that you'd like to unfollow, even just for today?"
"It could be stress, overthinking, pressure… anything that's been on repeat."

No need to go deep unless they want to.

Participant Experience
  • Write one thing they want to "unfollow" on a sticky note
  • Place it on the wall

Quick, personal, and engaging — that's it.

Bridging to Meditation

After they participate, gently connect the experience:

"Sometimes it's hard to just stop thinking about something, right?"
"Meditation can help create a little space between you and your thoughts."
"So instead of being pulled by everything happening in your mind, you can just see it more clearly."

Optional: "We have a 7-minute guided meditation here if you'd like to try it."

Volunteer Guidance
  • Keep the tone casual and welcoming
  • This is not about fixing anything — just noticing and stepping back
  • Let participants share as much or as little as they want
  • Avoid over-explaining — the experience should feel natural
  • Use this as a soft entry point into the meditation experience
Outcome
  • Creates immediate engagement
  • Helps people feel they are not alone in their experience
  • Opens the door to a deeper, experiential understanding through meditation
